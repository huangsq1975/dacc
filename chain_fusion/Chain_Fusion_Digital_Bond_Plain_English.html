Chapter One

Understanding "Fragmentation" Through a Real-Life Story

Imagine buying a flat in Shanghai while living in Hong Kong, paying in RMB. That's what this fragmentation feels like.

🏚 The Traditional Nightmare (Property Purchase Analogy)

You wire the money from Hong Kong to Shanghai (cash account moves). But the title deed at the Land Bureau is still in the previous owner's name (asset registry hasn't changed). Your Hong Kong bank has no idea the Shanghai Land Bureau exists, and vice versa. If you're a foreign buyer, you need to file separate reports with each country's authorities (regulatory reports done in parallel, independently).

The result: you need a solicitor, a notary, the bank's wire transfer desk, the Land Bureau, and the tax office — at least five departments — all coordinated manually by phone and paper.

📄 Digital Bonds Have the Same Problem

Buying an RMB digital bond is no different. Funds must travel via CIPS. Bond ownership must be updated at CMU or EuroClear. On-chain tokens must be transferred to your wallet. The money may pass through three banks via multi-bank clearing. And finally the whole transaction must be reported to regulators. Today, all five of these happen independently and are reconciled by hand.

❌ Without Chain Fusion
Like five departments each sending their own letter — one by courier, one by registered mail, one by fax. Who arrives first? Did all of them arrive? You have to phone each department individually to check. If one step is delayed, the whole transaction is left hanging in mid-air.
✅ With Chain Fusion
Like having a unified dispatch centre. It takes one package, replicates it into five copies, repackages each in the format that department understands, labels it in their language, delivers all five simultaneously, and only declares "transaction complete" once every single recipient has confirmed delivery.
Chapter Two

The Cast: Seven Worlds, Seven Languages

In a single RMB digital bond transaction, these seven parties each play a distinct role — and they cannot understand one another.

🏦
Issuing / Settlement Bank
Guardian of the money
Holds the RMB accounts of the issuer and investors. Responsible for KYC (identity checks), escrow of funds, and sending clearing instructions to CIPS.
📊
Securities Firm
The investor's front door
Underwrites the bond, manages investor subscriptions, and matches buyers and sellers in the secondary market. It is the entry point connecting investors to the whole system.
💱
CIPS
The RMB cross-border motorway
The Cross-border Interbank Payment System for RMB. All cross-border RMB flows must pass through this channel, which speaks ISO 20022 XML.
🏛
CMU (Hong Kong)
The "Land Registry" for bonds
The HKMA's Central Moneymarkets Unit. The official register of who holds which bond — equivalent to a property registry issuing title deeds for bonds.
🇪🇺
EuroClear
Europe's "Land Registry"
Europe's largest bond custody and clearing institution. Bond positions held by international investors are registered here. CMU and EuroClear must be linked.
⚙️
Chain Fusion
The unified coordination layer
The core of this system. Translates all formats, enforces compliance, triggers all steps simultaneously, and ensures all five worlds complete their actions at the same time.
🔗
Blockchain
The digital "safe" for tokens
Conflux, ETH, and others. Digital bond tokens are minted, transferred, and burned here. Smart contracts automatically execute coupon payment rules, with tamper-proof records.
🆕 What Is Multi-Bank Clearing?

When a cross-border RMB payment needs to travel from "Bank A in Hong Kong" to "Bank B in Shanghai," it may pass through: Correspondent Bank A → Clearing Bank B → Destination Bank C — three intermediate stops. Each bank records the transfer in its own ledger, while CIPS does the final settlement. Chain Fusion ensures that every confirmation along this multi-bank chain stays in real-time sync with the on-chain token status.

💡 What Is DvP?

DvP = Delivery versus Payment. This is the most important safety mechanism in financial markets: the bond ("delivery") and the cash ("payment") must settle at the exact same moment — neither side moves first. One of Chain Fusion's core values is guaranteeing DvP atomicity across chains and borders — either everything succeeds, or everything rolls back.

CIPS Multi-bank Clearing
Cross-border RMB funds travel through the multi-bank chain
CIPS receives the clearing instruction (pacs.008 XML, signed with SM2 Chinese cryptography). Funds hop through: Correspondent Bank → Clearing Bank → Destination Bank. Each hop is confirmed in that bank's ledger. CIPS returns a settlement receipt (camt.028).
📌 Plain-English equivalent: Like an RMB wire transfer that stops at several relay stations, each stamping a confirmation, before reaching its final destination.

📖 The Plain-English Summary

A traditional bond transaction is like needing to visit five different government offices in five different cities simultaneously — each speaks a different language, keeps different office hours, uses different forms, and expects you to personally verify that all their records match.

What Chain Fusion does is build an automated underground pipeline connecting all five offices. You submit one application at the entrance. In the background, it drives all five offices to complete their respective actions simultaneously, and only tells you "it's done" once every single one has confirmed. If any step fails, it automatically reverses every completed action and returns to the starting point.

That is why it's called "Fusion" — not replacing any existing system, but connecting all the fragmented systems into one coherent whole through a single intelligent pipeline.
